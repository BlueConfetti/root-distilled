# Root Faction Help / Hurt Cheatsheet

> Read this as: "What does the table do that helps or hurts this faction?"  
> Keep it political and practical. You do not need perfect counterplay, just enough to stop runaway curves.

---

### Marquise de Cat
##### Helping:
- Pressuring only one of their clearings — three Daylight actions stack cleanly into build, recruit, and battle there without forcing tradeoffs.
  - **Why**: With only three Daylight actions plus bird-card extras, threats split across distant clearings make them choose what to defend, where to build, and where to recruit. (`rule:6.5`, [Marquise profile § Common Pitfalls](factions/marquise.md#common-pitfalls), [Marquise profile § Playbook](factions/marquise.md#playbook))
- Ignoring early sawmills — wood compounds across turns and lands on the high-VP back-half of the build track.
  - **Why**: Each sawmill drops one wood per Birdsong with no per-clearing cap, and wood persists in a clearing until the Cat spends it through their ruled chain. Unanswered sawmills become stockpiles that fund late-track builds where the printed VP is highest. (`rule:6.4`, `rule:6.5.4.2`, `rule:6.5.4.3`, [Marquise profile § Scoring Engine](factions/marquise.md#scoring-engine), [BoardGameGeek § Marquise de Cat](strategy/sources/boardgamegeek--root-strategy-guide.md#marquise-de-cat))
- Letting their ruled clearings stay contiguous — never breaking the supply chain even when you can't take the clearing outright.
  - **Why**: Wood is paid from the build clearing, adjacent ruled clearings, OR clearings reached through any chain the Cat rules. Tying rule on one bridge clearing strands wood the Cat thought was spendable. (`rule:6.5.4.2`, `rule:2.5`, [Marquise profile § Common Pitfalls](factions/marquise.md#common-pitfalls), [Marquise profile § Playbook](factions/marquise.md#playbook))
- Letting them craft from a workshop in a suit you also need.
  - **Why**: A workshop activates as a crafting piece whose suit matches its clearing, so a single well-placed workshop covers a substantial slice of the deck. Crafting the same suit-card yourself — or destroying the workshop — denies the activation. (`rule:6.2.1`, `rule:4.1.1`, [Marquise profile § Card Priorities](factions/marquise.md#card-priorities), [BoardGameGeek § Marquise de Cat](strategy/sources/boardgamegeek--root-strategy-guide.md#marquise-de-cat))
- Leaving their recruiter count alone — recruiter scaling is non-linear.
  - **Why**: Recruit places one warrior at each recruiter once per turn, and on the Marquise board the recruiter track also covers the Evening draw bonuses. Each recruiter built compounds twice — more warriors per Recruit AND more cards every Evening. (`rule:6.5.3`, `rule:6.6`, [Marquise profile § Faction Rules and Abilities](factions/marquise.md#faction-rules-and-abilities), [Marquise profile § Turn Structure](factions/marquise.md#turn-structure), [BoardGameGeek § Marquise de Cat](strategy/sources/boardgamegeek--root-strategy-guide.md#marquise-de-cat))
- Battling them only when you can clear the warriors AND remove a building, token, or wood pile in the same battle.
  - **Why**: Buildings and tokens score 1 VP each on removal; warriors don't. Wood tokens are tokens too, so wood-pile removal also scores. Field Hospitals lets the Cat spend a matching card to redirect removed warriors to the keep clearing, so a warriors-only battle pays you nothing and feeds her recovery loop. Force the trade to include a scoring piece before committing the action. (`rule:3.2.1`, `rule:6.2.3`, `rule:4.3.6`, [Marquise profile § Faction Rules and Abilities](factions/marquise.md#faction-rules-and-abilities), [Marquise profile § Mechanic Clarifications](factions/marquise.md#mechanic-clarifications))
- Letting wood and warriors share the same clearing without testing it.
  - **Why**: Wood tokens count as Marquise tokens, so each wood removed in battle scores 1 VP. Defenders take warrior hits before buildings/tokens, but if you out-roll their warrior count you reach the wood pile. Clearings that look "too defended to attack" are often the most VP-dense targets. (`rule:3.2.1`, `rule:4.3.6`, [Marquise profile § Common Pitfalls](factions/marquise.md#common-pitfalls), [BoardGameGeek § Marquise de Cat](strategy/sources/boardgamegeek--root-strategy-guide.md#marquise-de-cat))
- Letting them rule five-plus clearings going into the closing turns — Royal Claim converts that footprint into a single-card 5-8 VP swing.
  - **Why**: `card:ROOT-92 (Base Game)` discards in Birdsong to score 1 VP per ruled clearing. With 25 warriors plus building density (each building counts toward rule), wide rule by mid-late game is plausible — and a single Royal Claim cashes the whole footprint. (`card:ROOT-92 (Base Game)`, `rule:2.5`, [Marquise profile § Playbook](factions/marquise.md#playbook), [Marquise profile § Card Priorities](factions/marquise.md#card-priorities), [BoardGameGeek § Marquise de Cat](strategy/sources/boardgamegeek--root-strategy-guide.md#marquise-de-cat))

##### Hurting:
- Sever wood-supply paths so a sawmill cannot reach an open building slot.
  - **Why**: Wood for a Build must come from the build clearing or clearings reached through clearings the Marquise rules — break that chain and the wood is stranded. (`rule:6.5.4.2`, [Marquise profile § Common Pitfalls](factions/marquise.md#common-pitfalls), [Marquise profile § Faction Rules and Abilities](factions/marquise.md#faction-rules-and-abilities))
- Tip rule away from her sawmill clearings on your turn — Birdsong still places wood there, but Pay Cost requires her to rule the wood-source clearing along a contiguous chain.
  - **Why**: Adding warriors until your total exceeds the Marquise's warriors+buildings — or until you tie, breaking rule for both — freezes that wood for funding. (`rule:6.4`, `rule:6.5.4.2`, `rule:2.5`, [Marquise profile § Turn Structure](factions/marquise.md#turn-structure), [Marquise profile § Common Pitfalls](factions/marquise.md#common-pitfalls))
- Move warriors into the keep clearing — placement is blocked, but moves aren't.
  - **Why**: The Keep only blocks placement by enemies, not movement, so contesting rule there pressures the Marquise's revival hub and the clearing every Field Hospital warrior returns to. (`rule:6.2.2`, `rule:6.2.3`, `faq:keep-move`, [Marquise profile § Faction Rules and Abilities](factions/marquise.md#faction-rules-and-abilities), [Marquise profile § Mechanic Clarifications](factions/marquise.md#mechanic-clarifications))
- When picking which Marquise building to destroy, prioritize recruiters.
  - **Why**: Recruit places one warrior at each recruiter once per turn, and the recruiter track on the Marquise board also covers the Evening draw bonuses. Killing recruiters chokes both the warrior pump and the card draw that funds Field Hospitals and bird-card extra actions. (`rule:6.5.3`, `rule:6.6`, `rule:6.2.3`, [Marquise profile § Scoring Engine](factions/marquise.md#scoring-engine), [Marquise profile § Common Pitfalls](factions/marquise.md#common-pitfalls), [BoardGameGeek § Marquise de Cat](strategy/sources/boardgamegeek--root-strategy-guide.md#marquise-de-cat))
- Block crafting in the suits she actually needs — rabbit especially, for Command Warren, Cobbler, and Better Burrow Bank.
  - **Why**: Crafting requires activating workshops by clearing suit, and her best persistent rabbit-cost effects (`card:ROOT-63 (Base Game)`, `card:ROOT-60 (Base Game)`, `card:ROOT-55 (Base Game)`) all gate on rabbit access. Destroying her rabbit workshop, or denying rule on the rabbit clearing where she'd rebuild, defers those persistent effects indefinitely. (`rule:4.1.1`, `card:ROOT-63 (Base Game)`, `card:ROOT-60 (Base Game)`, `card:ROOT-55 (Base Game)`, [Marquise profile § Card Priorities](factions/marquise.md#card-priorities), [BoardGameGeek § Marquise de Cat](strategy/sources/boardgamegeek--root-strategy-guide.md#marquise-de-cat))
- Watch which suits she has workshops in — destroy the workshop in the suit you most fear her crafting.
  - **Why**: A workshop's suit matches its clearing; destroying the workshop scores you 1 VP AND eliminates her ability to craft cards requiring that suit until she rebuilds (one action plus wood). (`rule:4.1.1`, `rule:3.2.1`, `rule:6.5.4`, [Marquise profile § Card Priorities](factions/marquise.md#card-priorities), [BoardGameGeek § Marquise de Cat](strategy/sources/boardgamegeek--root-strategy-guide.md#marquise-de-cat))
- If she crafts Royal Claim, kill rule on her peripheral clearings before she can fire it.
  - **Why**: `card:ROOT-92 (Base Game)` discards in Birdsong to score 1 VP per clearing the Cat rules. Late-game, an incursion that ties rule on two or three peripheral clearings costs her 2-3 Royal Claim VP — worth even an action that "wastes" your turn. (`rule:2.5`, `card:ROOT-92 (Base Game)`, [Marquise profile § Playbook](factions/marquise.md#playbook), [Marquise profile § Card Priorities](factions/marquise.md#card-priorities))
- Bait her into "wasted" wood — wood placed in a clearing she'll lose rule of next turn cannot be spent for any future Build.
  - **Why**: Birdsong wood placement is automatic per sawmill regardless of rule, but Pay Cost requires the wood-source clearing to be ruled by the Marquise along a contiguous chain at the moment of Build. If she loses rule on the source, the remaining wood is stranded. (`rule:6.4`, `rule:6.5.4.2`, [Marquise profile § Common Pitfalls](factions/marquise.md#common-pitfalls), [BoardGameGeek § Marquise de Cat](strategy/sources/boardgamegeek--root-strategy-guide.md#marquise-de-cat))
- Bring Brutal Tactics when attacking dense Marquise stacks; the 1-VP gift is small versus the buildings you crack open.
  - **Why**: `card:ROOT-58 (Base Game)` deals 1 extra hit as attacker but gives the defender 1 VP. Removing one Marquise building scores you 1 VP — netting zero on that piece, but each additional warrior or building removed in the same battle nets full value. (`card:ROOT-58 (Base Game)`, `rule:3.2.1`, [Marquise profile § Card Priorities](factions/marquise.md#card-priorities))
- As Woodland, target sympathy at clearings adjacent to the keep, not the keep itself.
  - **Why**: Sympathy can't be placed in the keep clearing, but adjacent-to-keep sympathy taxes the Marquise with Outrage every march back through, draining the cards she needs for Field Hospitals. A revolt that destroys a building on her wood-chain spine also strands wood deeper in her network. (`rule:6.2.2`, `faction:woodland$8.2.6`, `rule:6.2.3`, `rule:6.5.4.2`, [Marquise profile § Threat Factions](factions/marquise.md#threat-factions), [BoardGameGeek § Marquise de Cat](strategy/sources/boardgamegeek--root-strategy-guide.md#marquise-de-cat))
- As Woodland, place sympathy on her wood-chain spine, not on her isolated outposts — the threat alone constrains her routing.
  - **Why**: Build's Pay Cost requires her to rule a contiguous chain to the wood source, and a future revolt would break that chain plus remove a building. Outrage charges her cards for every March through sympathetic spine clearings on top. (`rule:6.5.4.2`, `faction:woodland$8.4.1`, `faction:woodland$8.2.6`, [Marquise profile § Threat Factions](factions/marquise.md#threat-factions), [BoardGameGeek § Woodland Alliance](strategy/sources/boardgamegeek--root-strategy-guide.md#woodland-alliance))
- After breaking her warriors in a Marquise clearing, follow through to remove the building under Defenseless on the same turn.
  - **Why**: Field Hospitals warps her warriors to the keep mid-battle, and the now-empty clearing is Defenseless to the next attack — +1 hit, no warriors absorbing. Walking away gifts the Cat a free recycle (warriors back at keep, building intact); a second battle action cashes the building. (`rule:4.3.5.2`, `rule:6.2.3`, `faq:ambush-keep`, [Marquise profile § Faction Rules and Abilities](factions/marquise.md#faction-rules-and-abilities))

##### Awareness:
- Field Hospitals fires immediately during an ambush and can save the battle from auto-ending — warriors warp to the keep before the "no remaining warriors" check (`rule:6.2.3`, `rule:4.3.1.2`, `faq:field-hospitals-ambush-keep`).

### Eyrie Dynasties
##### Helping:
- Letting roosts proliferate, especially across every suit — each roost is a Recruit anchor and an Evening VP tick, and full suit coverage keeps every Decree column resolvable.
  - **Why**: Evening VP scales directly with roost count, and Recruit and Build both require a suit-matched clearing, so concentration in one suit is far less dangerous to them than a balanced spread. (`faction:eyrie$7.5.2.1`, `faction:eyrie$7.5.2.4`, `faction:eyrie$7.6.1`)

##### Hurting:
- Strip one suit out of their Decree — make every matching clearing fail its required action (no roost for Recruit, no warriors for Battle, no rule for Build, no origin for Move).
  - **Why**: Any unfulfillable Decree card forces turmoil, costing one VP per bird card on the Decree (Loyal Viziers included) and dumping the column. (`faction:eyrie$7.5.2`, `faction:eyrie$7.7.1`)
- Bait forced Battle Decrees into dug-in defenders — they can't decline a battle they're able to initiate, so a strong defender in that suit absorbs their tempo.
  - **Why**: Resolve the Decree mandates each action ("you must take the action listed... if you cannot fully take an action, you immediately fall into turmoil"); Battle says "initiate a battle in any clearing whose suit matches the card suit," with no opt-out. (`faction:eyrie$7.5.2`, `faction:eyrie$7.5.2.3`)

##### Awareness:
- Current leader changes which counter is correct: Despot taxes building/token loss, Commander adds an attacker hit, Charismatic doubles Recruit, Builder removes Disdain for Trade (`faction:eyrie$7.8.1`, `faction:eyrie$7.8.2`, `faction:eyrie$7.8.3`, `faction:eyrie$7.8.4`).

### Woodland Alliance
##### Helping:
- Moving warriors into sympathetic clearings — every entry adds a matching card from your hand to the Supporters stack, or forces you to reveal your hand and lets them draw blind from the deck if you have no match.
  - **Why**: Outrage triggers on moving warriors in OR removing sympathy; with no matching card, the moving player must show their entire hand and the Alliance draws from the deck instead. (`faction:woodland$8.2.6`)
- Letting them place their first base — the 5-card Supporters cap disappears, and Train and Evening Recruit both come online for that suit.
  - **Why**: Cap of 5 only applies with no bases; once any base is on the map, the stack is unlimited, and Train requires a base of matching suit. (`faction:woodland$8.2.4.1`, `faction:woodland$8.5.3`, `faction:woodland$8.6.1`)
- Battling them in sympathetic clearings while leaving the sympathy token standing — they keep Guerrilla War's high-roll defense and you still hand over an Outrage supporter for moving in.
  - **Why**: As defender the Alliance deals hits equal to the higher roll; entering still triggers Outrage from movement. (`faction:woodland$8.2.2`, `faction:woodland$8.2.6`)

##### Hurting:
- Park 3+ warriors in any clearing they want to make sympathetic — Martial Law adds a second matching supporter cost to Spread there.
  - **Why**: Spread Sympathy requires an extra matching supporter when the target clearing has 3+ warriors of another player. (`faction:woodland$8.4.2.2.1`)
- Snipe a base the turn it lands — destruction discards every matching supporter and removes half their officers (rounded up) in one stroke.
  - **Why**: Removing a base auto-discards all supporters of that suit and halves officers; if it was their only base, the 5-cap returns. (`faction:woodland$8.2.5`)

##### Awareness:
- Spread Sympathy requires adjacency to existing sympathy — except when no sympathy is on the map, in which case the Alliance can seed the first token in any clearing (`faction:woodland$8.4.2.1`).

### Vagabond
##### Helping:
- Craft suited items he can Aid out of for the price of one matching card.
  - **Why**: Aid lets the Vagabond exhaust an item and give a clearing-suit card to take an item from your Crafted box; if the Aid count meets the next Allied-track threshold, he also advances the relationship marker and scores the new space's VP. (`faction:vagabond$9.5.4`, `faction:vagabond$9.2.9.1.2`)

##### Hurting:
- Battle him in his clearing whenever his sword is exhausted or damaged — every hit damages an item and he is defenseless without one undamaged sword.
  - **Why**: Each hit forces him to damage one undamaged item, and with no face-up undamaged `item:sword` he is defenseless. (`faction:vagabond$9.2.7`)

##### Awareness:
- Hostile status is one-directional and Vagabond-controlled — it flips when *he* removes one of your warriors (whether he attacked you or his defender hits killed warriors during your attack), not when you remove his items or pieces; you have no decision point and no consent (`faction:vagabond$9.2.9.3`).
- Hostile is effectively permanent — Aid cannot reverse it, only forming a Coalition with you can, and while Hostile he scores +1 VP Infamy per piece of yours removed in battle on his turn on top of standard building/token VP (`faction:vagabond$9.2.9.3.1`, `faction:vagabond$9.2.9.3.3`, `faction:vagabond$9.2.9.3.4`).

### Lizard Cult
##### Helping:
- Discarding or spending cards of one suit in volume — it sets that suit as their Outcast and triggers Hated, cutting each conspiracy by one acolyte.
  - **Why**: All discards/spends go to the Lost Souls pile, which determines Outcast in Birdsong; repeats flip it Hated, and Perform Conspiracies pays one fewer acolyte while Hated. (`faction:cult$10.2.6`, `faction:cult$10.4.1`, `faction:cult$10.4.3`)

##### Hurting:
- Remove gardens early — every garden lost forces a random card discard and shrinks the next Score VP for that suit.
  - **Why**: Fear of the Faithful makes the Cult discard a random card per garden removed, and Score reads the rightmost-empty Gardens space, so removing a garden returns it to the track and lowers the next Score's VP. (`faction:cult$10.2.5`, `faction:cult$10.5.3`)
- Avoid attacking the Cult unless you can also break a garden — your hits feed Acolytes, but garden removal punishes them.
  - **Why**: Revenge converts removed defending warriors into Acolytes, so battle that only kills warriors is a net positive for them. (`faction:cult$10.2.3`)
- Before Cult Birdsong, move warriors out of clearings matching the projected Outcast and don't park lone buildings there — Convert and Sanctify only fire in Outcast clearings.
  - **Why**: Convert and Sanctify both target Outcast clearings, and Crusade only moves/battles from one; vacating those clearings denies their conspiracy targets entirely. (`faction:cult$10.4.3.1`, `faction:cult$10.4.3.2`, `faction:cult$10.4.3.3`)

##### Awareness:
- Score VP scales with gardens-of-that-suit on the map — a single dominant suit is the Cult's best Score engine (`faction:cult$10.5.3`).

### Riverfolk Company
##### Helping:
- Let one player become the sole regular customer; concentrated demand stabilizes their funds and Dividend scoring.
  - **Why**: Score Dividends pays 1 VP per 2 funds whenever any trade post is on the map, so steady payments compound into VP. (`faction:riverfolk$11.4.2`)

##### Hurting:
- Destroy any trade post the moment their funds spike — Trade Disruption halves their funds (rounded up) and the post is gone for good.
  - **Why**: Trade Disruption removes half their funds rounded up and removes the trade post from the game permanently. (`faction:riverfolk$11.2.5.1`)
- Refuse mercenary deals at silly prices — make them lower the marker before you pay, since they reset costs every Evening.
  - **Why**: Set Costs lets them move each service marker every Evening, so price discipline forces them to drop. (`faction:riverfolk$11.6.2`)
- Don't rule the clearings adjacent to rivers you want to deny — they can only Establish Trade Post in a clearing ruled by a player.
  - **Why**: Establish Trade Post requires choosing a clearing ruled by any player, and spends two of that ruler's funds. (`faction:riverfolk$11.5.6.1`, `faction:riverfolk$11.5.6.2`)

##### Awareness:
- Riverfolk treat rivers as paths — any river-touching clearing is one move from any other river clearing, regardless of rule (`faction:riverfolk$11.2.2`).

### Underground Duchy
##### Helping:
- Battling tunnels without also destroying citadels or markets — they re-place tunnels and lose nothing permanent.
  - **Why**: Tunnels can be removed and re-placed by the Duchy, but only building destruction triggers Price of Failure. (`faction:duchy$12.2.4`, `faction:duchy$12.2.3`)

##### Hurting:
- Destroy citadels and markets, not tunnels — each building-removal event permanently burns one of their nine crowns.
  - **Why**: Price of Failure returns the highest-rank swayed minister AND permanently removes its crown from the game whenever any Duchy buildings are removed. (`faction:duchy$12.2.3`)
- Spread building removals across multiple turns or battles — they only lose one minister per event, so separated hits compound the loss.
  - **Why**: Price of Failure triggers once per "any number of Duchy buildings… removed" event; one event loses one minister regardless of how many buildings fell, so spacing the destruction multiplies the cost. (`faction:duchy$12.2.3`)
- Pressure them before they sway lords — early Duchy is fragile, and a lord-rank crown lost to Price of Failure is the most expensive piece of permanent damage you can deal.
  - **Why**: Price of Failure removes the highest-rank swayed minister first (lord, then noble, then squire) and the crown is gone permanently, capping their late-game scoring. (`faction:duchy$12.2.3`)

##### Awareness:
- Duchess of Mud auto-scores 2 VP per turn while all three tunnels are on the map — destroying any tunnel breaks the bonus (`faction:duchy$12.5.2.7`).
- The Burrow is permanently Duchy-ruled and inaccessible to opponents — every Duchy turn places at least one warrior there for free, and Dig pulls from that pool, so warrior pressure on tunneled clearings is continuous (`faction:duchy$12.2.2`, `faction:duchy$12.4`, `faction:duchy$12.5.1.5`).

### Corvid Conspiracy
##### Helping:
- Guessing face-down plots without a sure read — wrong guesses just hand the matching card straight to the Corvids.
  - **Why**: Failed Exposure gives the shown card to the Corvids; they only score VP via Flip Plots, not on your miss. (`faction:corvid$13.2.4`)
- Letting face-up plots accumulate before you remove them — every flip scores 1 VP per face-up plot on the map, the newly flipped one included.
  - **Why**: Flip Plots scores one VP per face-up plot token on the map (including the one just flipped), so each unflipped plot inflates every future flip. (`faction:corvid$13.4.2`)
- Leaving a face-up extortion token alive in a clearing you keep pieces in — they draw a bonus card every Evening it stays up.
  - **Why**: Drawing in Evening is one card plus one per face-up extortion on the map, and each flip already stole a random card from you. (`faction:corvid$13.6.2`, `faction:corvid$13.7.3`)

##### Hurting:
- Battle into clearings with plot tokens to remove them outright — no guess required, and the Corvids never score the flip VP.
  - **Why**: Tokens can be battled and removed by powers that remove pieces, so combat removal denies the Corvid the Flip Plots VP entirely. (`rule:2.5`, `rule:4.3`, `faction:corvid$13.4.2`)
- Prioritize removing face-up extortion tokens early — each one denied is both a card you keep and a card the Corvids do not draw.
  - **Why**: Extortion's flip steals a random card and its face-up presence grants an extra Evening draw, compounding card-economy loss every turn it stands. (`faction:corvid$13.7.3`, `faction:corvid$13.6.2`)

##### Awareness:
- A face-up snare locks pieces from being placed in or moved from its clearing — warriors parked there are stranded until the snare comes off (`faction:corvid$13.7.2`).

### Lord of the Hundreds
##### Helping:
- Crafting items into your own Crafted Items box where the Hundreds can reach you — Looters siphons one item per battle if they rule the clearing at the end.
  - **Why**: At start of battle as attacker, the Hundreds may declare loot if the defender has a crafted item; if they rule the clearing at end of battle they take one item from that box. Declaring loot means they deal no rolled hits that battle (Wrathful and other extra hits still apply); the Vagabond cannot be looted. (`faction:warlord$14.2.5`)
- Stacking pieces in the warlord's clearing while they are Wrathful — they deal a bonus hit on top of the roll when attacking there.
  - **Why**: While Wrathful, as attacker in battle in the warlord's clearing, the Hundreds deal an extra hit. (`faction:warlord$14.7.8`)

##### Hurting:
- Place any warrior in a clearing the Hundreds rule with a Hundreds piece — that clearing no longer counts toward Oppress scoring this Evening.
  - **Why**: Oppress scores only for clearings the Hundreds rule that have a Hundreds piece and no enemy pieces. (`faction:warlord$14.6.2`)
- Kill the warlord in battle to strand the mood and stall Advance the Warlord — until Anoint replaces him next Birdsong, the Hundreds lose their Prowess-many Advance actions, the per-Prowess Recruit dump, and any "in the warlord's clearing" mood ability.
  - **Why**: Birdsong Recruit places Prowess-many warriors in the warlord's clearing, and Advance the Warlord plus most Mood effects key off it; the warlord can only be removed in battle, and is replaced by Anoint as Birdsong's third step. (`faction:warlord$14.2.2`, `faction:warlord$14.4.2`, `faction:warlord$14.4.3`, `faction:warlord$14.5.3`)
- Contest or break rule of the warlord's stronghold clearings — without rule they cannot Build new strongholds, throttling craft activations and per-stronghold Recruit.
  - **Why**: Build during Command places a stronghold in a clearing the Hundreds rule by spending a card; Recruit during Birdsong places one warrior per stronghold there. (`faction:warlord$14.5.2.3`, `faction:warlord$14.4.2`)
- Clear mob tokens off the map before Birdsong so the next Raze fires in zero clearings and the spread roll has nothing to extend from.
  - **Why**: Raze in Birdsong only fires in clearings with mob tokens, and the post-Raze spread roll only places a new mob in a matching clearing adjacent to an existing one — with no mobs at all, the spread roll fires but places nothing. (`faction:warlord$14.4.1`)

##### Awareness:
- Once a Hoard track (Command or Prowess) is full, every additional matching item gained scores them 1 free VP — Looters, ruin loot, and crafting all become VP engines once the relevant track fills (`faction:warlord$14.2.4.1`).
- Mob tokens are persistent — once placed, they fire Raze every Birdsong until removed (`faction:warlord$14.4.1`).

### Keepers in Iron
##### Helping:
- Letting a Keeper warrior and a relic share a clearing — Devout Knights then ignores the first hit you'd deal, blunting your battle.
  - **Why**: With at least one relic and one Keeper in the clearing of battle, Keepers ignore the first hit taken; the threshold is one of each, not a stack. (`rule:15.2.4`)
- Leaving a relic in an adjacent forest when they rule the clearing and have a Keeper there — that's the exact Battle-then-Delve trigger, and ducking the fight just hands them the relic.
  - **Why**: Delve flips and moves an adjacent-forest relic into a ruled clearing with a Keeper warrior. (`rule:15.5.2.2`)

##### Hurting:
- When you battle into a clearing where a Keeper has just delved a relic, prioritize removing the relic over warriors — every relic an enemy removes scores them 2 VP total.
  - **Why**: Prized Trophies grants the remover one extra VP on top of the standard piece-removal point, and the post-Delve / pre-Recover window is the table's main shot at the relic. (`rule:15.2.5`)
- Break their rule in clearings adjacent to the delve forest, or matching the recover clearing's suit — falling under the relic's value forces them to discard the Retinue card they just used.
  - **Why**: Both Delve and Recover force a Retinue discard if ruled clearings (adjacent-to-forest for Delve, suit-matching for Recover) are fewer than the relic's value. (`rule:15.5.2.2.2`, `rule:15.5.2.3.3`)
- Pressure clearings with a waystation and matching relic — Recover happens there, and starving rule on matching-suit clearings forces a Retinue discard each time, while removing waystations also cuts their per-waystation card draw.
  - **Why**: Recover requires a waystation matching the relic's type in the same clearing; rule isn't a Recover prerequisite, but ruling fewer matching-suit clearings than the relic's value forces a Retinue-card discard, and Evening draw equals 1 + waystations on map. (`rule:15.5.2.3.1`, `rule:15.5.2.3.3`, `rule:15.6.3`)

##### Awareness:
- Retinue is open information — the leftmost-to-rightmost columns telegraph the suit and order of this turn's Move / Battle-then-Delve / Move-or-Recover (`rule:15.5.2`).

### Lilypad Diaspora
##### Helping:
- Negotiating Militant enclaves back to Peaceful instead of fighting through them — you turn off Retaliate and the Rally warrior pump, but you fatten Integrate's scoring base.
  - **Why**: Negotiations let any enemy with faction pieces in the clearing flip Militant to Peaceful (free if no third party rules the enclave; otherwise you must give the ruler a card), restoring the suited side for Integrate scoring next Evening. (`faction:diaspora$16.2.5`, `faction:diaspora$16.6.2`)
- Building in their suited clearings without contesting rule — they place Peaceful enclaves only where they rule, and each one becomes an Integrate VP every Evening.
  - **Why**: Settle requires rule to drop a Peaceful enclave, and Integrate scores 1 VP per matching Peaceful enclave for one card spent. (`faction:diaspora$16.5.1`, `faction:diaspora$16.6.2`)

##### Hurting:
- Attack Peaceful enclaves only when you can stomach a board-wide flip — Fears Come to Pass flips every Peaceful enclave sharing a clearing with any of your pieces (across the whole map) to Militant and spawns a warrior at each.
  - **Why**: A battle attacking a defending Peaceful enclave (or any Peaceful enclave removal outside battle) triggers a board-wide cascade: the rule reads "flip all Peaceful enclaves with that enemy's pieces," not just the battle clearing. (`faction:diaspora$16.2.4`)
- Keep contesting rule in their suited clearings so Integrate stalls — flip suited enclaves to Militant via Provoke-bait or removal so they cannot score them.
  - **Why**: Integrate cannot score the frog suit, and Militant enclaves replace the printed suit entirely, zeroing their VP for that clearing. (`faction:diaspora$16.2.1.1`, `faction:diaspora$16.6.2`)
- Remove their warriors from Militant enclaves before Evening so Retaliate forces a no-defender battle, or skip Negotiations to leave them locked into mandatory fights.
  - **Why**: Retaliate compels a battle at every Militant enclave each Evening, bleeding warriors when the enclave is undefended. (`faction:diaspora$16.6.1`)
- Deny river clearings whenever Frog Dominance is on the table — any player who has activated it wins by ruling at least 2 enclaves on the river at start of their Birdsong.
  - **Why**: Frog Dominance is a shared sudden-death wincon, so any rival can race the Diaspora to the river-enclave threshold. (`faction:diaspora$16.2.2.1`)

##### Awareness:
- Diaspora Draw scales with Peaceful enclaves on the map — +1 at 2-3, +2 at 4-7, +3 at 8 or more (`faction:diaspora$16.6.3`).

### Twilight Council
##### Helping:
- Building or placing tokens at their assembly clearings — every assembly co-located with enemy buildings/tokens scales Oversee.
  - **Why**: Oversee scores 1/2/3/4 VP based on the count of Governing assemblies sharing a clearing with enemy buildings or tokens; warriors do not count. (`faction:council$17.5.4`)

##### Hurting:
- Remove or contest assemblies before Evening — every removed assembly costs them a Loyalist, and unruled ones flip back to Closed.
  - **Why**: When an enemy removes an assembly, the Council removes 1 Loyalist; Sleep only flips enemy-ruled assemblies to Closed, so contesting rule denies Adjourn's Governing flip. (`faction:council$17.2.2`, `faction:council$17.4.1`, `faction:council$17.5.3`)
- Keep buildings and tokens out of clearings that hold Governing assemblies — Oversee zeroes out without enemy structures co-located.
  - **Why**: Oversee scores only on Governing assemblies sharing a clearing with enemy buildings or tokens; warrior presence alone is worthless to them. (`faction:council$17.5.4`)
- Avoid attacking other enemies at Council assembly clearings — Peacekeepers drags Council warriors in as extra defenders for the other faction.
  - **Why**: When an enemy chooses another enemy as defender at an assembly, Council warriors are added as defending warriors and only take hits once the chosen defender's warriors are removed. Exception: Peacekeepers is ignored if the Vagabond is defending; the Council also cannot ambush or use battle effects in this role. (`faction:council$17.2.5`)

##### Awareness:
- Council Assemble keeps the revealed card if they rule the destination clearing, and discards it otherwise — disputing rule at telegraphed assembly clearings taxes their Birdsong card economy (`faction:council$17.3.3`).

### Knaves of the Deepwood
##### Helping:
- Battling a Captain's clearing without enough hits to clear all Skunks first — surplus hits are wasted because Captains can't be hit until Skunks are gone.
  - **Why**: Captains can only be hit in battle if no Skunks remain in the clearing, so insufficient hits never threaten the Captain. (`faction:knaves$18.2.5`)
- Leaving defenders next to forests holding no Prisoners — Have at Thee converts a hit into a Prisoner and (if the Acting Captain is in the clearing) places acclaim there.
  - **Why**: When a Captain attacks in battle, the Knaves take 1 defending warrior they hit as a Prisoner — moving it to an adjacent forest with no Prisoners — and if the Acting Captain is still in the battle clearing at the end, acclaim is placed there. (`faction:knaves$18.2.6`)

##### Hurting:
- Remove acclaim on the turn before Knaves Evening — you deny the Mock the Powerful VP and pick which forest the forced Skunk wastes itself in.
  - **Why**: Mock the Powerful scores 1 VP per 2 acclaim, and Run Away lets the removing enemy choose the adjacent forest where the Knaves must place a Skunk. (`faction:knaves$18.6.1`, `faction:knaves$18.2.7`)
- Skip crafting items you don't urgently need — every face-up item in your Crafted Items box is one Filch away from the Knaves' Stash.
  - **Why**: Filch can take 1 item from an enemy's Crafted Items box, and items in the Stash power Item Actions like Assault, Dash, Skirmish, and Nab. (`faction:knaves$18.5.1.3`, `faction:knaves$18.5.1.4`)
- Battle into a Captain's forest before they Ready — fewer Skunks defending means you can remove the Captain itself and force a Birdsong action down.
  - **Why**: Captains cannot be placed except in setup or via Ready, so removing one off-map costs the Knaves an action down on their next Birdsong. (`faction:knaves$18.2.5`, `faction:knaves$18.4`)

##### Awareness:
- Face-down Captains are benched until Take It Easy — their forest is dormant for the rest of the cycle, so threats from that quadrant are paused (`faction:knaves$18.5.2`, `faction:knaves$18.6.3`).

## Quick Table Rule

- If someone has an engine, break the engine.
- If someone has tempo, make them spend actions defensively.
- If someone scores passively, force interaction.
- If someone needs a specific suit or clearing, deny that exact thing.
- If you are not sure what to do, hurt the leader without feeding the second-place faction for free.
